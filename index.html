Woxsen Olympiad 2025 - Problem-Solving Event

Test Structure


The Olympiad consists of 5 carefully crafted sections to evaluate every participant's strengths:

  1. Quantitative Aptitude
    • Focuses on numerical ability and mathematical problem-solving.
    • Topics include: Arithmetic, Number Systems, Percentages, Profit and loss
  2. Logical Reasoning
    • Assesses critical thinking and logical analysis skills.
    • Topics include: Patterns, Series, Coding-Decoding, Blood Relations, and Syllogisms.
  3. Puzzles
    • Tests problem-solving skills through logical and analytical puzzles.
    • Examples: Seating Arrangements, Grid Problems, and Brain Teasers.
  4. Verbal Reasoning
    • Evaluates language comprehension and verbal ability.
    • Topics include: Reading Comprehension, Sentence Correction, Vocabulary, and Para Jumbles.
  5. Psychometric Behavior Analysis
    • Analyzes behavioral traits, decision-making ability, and mindset.
    • Aims to provide insights into personality and problem-handling approaches.

Exam Details:


Will be conducted in 3 rounds:
  1. Round 1: Basic Aptitude and Reasoning test
  2. Round 2: Teamwork and decision making round
  3. Round 3: Critical Analysis Round

Round 1 Assessment:

  • Quantitative Aptitude: Focuses on numerical ability and mathematical problem-solving.
  • Logical Reasoning: Assesses critical thinking and logical analysis skills.
  • Puzzles: Tests problem-solving skills through logical and analytical puzzles.
  • Verbal Reasoning: Evaluates language comprehension and verbal ability.
  • Psychometric Behavior: Analyzes behavioral traits, decision-making ability, and mindset.

Test Duration: Total Test Duration: 60 Minutes

Marking Scheme: Each question carries 1 mark. Negative marking for incorrect answers.

Round 2: Decision making (Team task) around 15-30 min to solve

Round 3: Critical Analysis with time duration of 30 min
